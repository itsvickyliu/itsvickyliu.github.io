My Partner in Crime

Nov 2020

Writing has always been an essential part of my life. Even before I began studying characters, I "wrote," orating as my mom transcribed. From imagining the fate of autumn leaves to playing with a dancing tree in the wind, my childhood journals kept a record of my pure and unfiltered curiosity about the world.

Soon, I was no longer satisfied with merely asking questions: I attempted to answer them by writing fiction. In fifth grade, while learning about the formation of clouds in science class, a friend and I wondered what living above the clouds would be like. To answer this question, we wrote a novel about a kingdom in the sky. As our notebook, torn and tattered from months of writing and editing, was passed around the class, I realized my power to create whole new worlds with words.

For three consecutive years since fifth grade, I traveled from China to the U.S. for summer camps. Journaling in my notebook encouraged me to reflect on cultural differences and develop an open mindset. That journal accompanied me as I tried weird-looking toast with pepper and salt, attended flying trapeze sessions five times a week to film a perfect clip of my knee hang trick, and directed a thriller whose plot I barely understood.

As I entered high school, I wrote about studying at an all-girls school, learning about feminism, and questioning the correlation between female objectification and leg shaving. This time, I was ready to share my thoughts through screenplays and films. My reflections on feminism led to a sarcastic film called Who Belongs, in which I proposed feminism as a liberation movement for women and men. After the film premiered at the All American High School Film Festival, writing remained my outlet for curiosity, my wonderland of imagination, and my connection with foreign culture, but moreover it had become a bridge between me and mass audiences.

Writing, a symphony between thoughts and words, is my precious talent and my "partner in crime" as I continue my adventure through the world.
