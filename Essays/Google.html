Google Case Study (Management & Technology Summer Institute)

Jul 2020

Founded in 1998, Google started as a company that returns high quality search results to its end users. Google establishes itself as a strong player in the search engine industry because of its uniquely positioned value proposition, improved revenue streams, and stable cost structures.

Google creates value by offering superior search performance and customization for its end users and licensees. Google's and its licensees' end users can efficiently find information using the search engine with the PageRank algorithm. Early search service companies, such as Yahoo!, rank search results based on the frequency of keyword appearance in page contents. However, their search engines began to return irrelevant pages, or “spams,” to users as website developers increasingly exploit the search algorithm by repeating keywords on their websites. Unlike search service companies such as Yahoo!, Google's PageRank algorithm shifts from relying on keyword frequency to the amount of inbound links, ranking websites that are more frequently referenced by other websites higher. The PageRank algorithm also allows Google to increase its index of web pages rapidly, supporting it to index 1 billion web pages a year after the first-round funding. The virtuous cycle of employing a reliable and scalable search algorithm and increasing the web page index enables Google to become a formidable search service company that offers its end users and licensees high quality search results that surpass the performance of its competitors. At the same time, refinements to Google's search algorithm ensure a customized search experience for its end users. By launching Personalized Search, Google is able to return its end users with customized page rankings based on their search history and prior clicks. By introducing the contextual paid listing service named AdSense and displaying paid listings while taking their relevance into consideration, Google provides users with advertisements suited to their search interests.

In addition, Google creates value for the advertisers by offering superior advertising performance through Google Analytics and geographic targeting systems. By providing software analytics tools such as Google Analytics, Google allows advertisers to track the performance of their advertising keywords, allowing them to later cost-efficiently invest in keywords that yield more sales. With a paid-listing click-through rate that is twice as high as its competitor Yahoo!, Google provides a more effective and higher quality advertisement platform for advertisers. Moreover, the geographic targeting system that Google has allows it to serve small and midsize businesses around the world. By pushing related advertisements to end users in the same geographical region, Google supports a new channel for local businesses to connect with potential customers.

Google's revenue streams mainly come from paid-listing advertisements, and a small fraction of the revenue is generated from licensing its search algorithms to other companies. Early paid listing companies such as Overture display text advertisements next to search results and use “cost-per-click” (CPC) auctions to yield revenue. Overture succeeds because it creates value for advertisers by providing a more effective channel for marketing, partners with large portals to reach a substantial amount of end users and thus attract advertisers, and has a sustainable revenue stream from advertisers who vigorously bid high prices for keywords and top positions. Compared to Overture's business model, Google specifically improved its revenue streams. Google weighs CPC bids based on an advertisement's expected and actual click-through rate (CTR) so that relevant advertisements secure top positions, that Google receives promising revenue from advertisers with high CTR advertisements, and that the business' revenue is thus maximized. Moreover, unlike Overture who simply displays advertisements in the order based on bids, Google takes search relevance into consideration. Therefore, Google's text advertisements have higher CTRs, incentivizing advertisers to pay higher bids for more effective marketing. In addition to the improvements mentioned above, Google is able to charge advertisers higher because of its large paid-listing advertiser base. According to the case study, one of the four factors that influence paid listing revenues is the average CPC, which increases as the size of paid-listing advertiser base increases. With advertisers two to three times more than that of Overture, Google is able to charge higher CPC than most of its competitors. These factors ultimately result in Google's dramatic profitability.

The minimized and stable cost structures, along with profitable revenue streams, support Google to become a sustainable and leading business in the industry. During the early stages, the highly valued service that Google provides to its end users allows it to spend less on marketing while becoming the ninth-largest U.S. website. Thus, Google spent the money saved from sales and marketing to R&D, developing and refining search algorithms that further improve the user experience. Another effort of Google minimizing its cost structures is having its own low-cost server architecture. As Google scales its business, the majority of costs result from traffic acquisition. By annually spending around 20% of its revenue on traffic acquisition, not only does Google gain a substantial amount of traffic and thus increase its attractiveness to advertisers, but it also ensures that the cost isn't burdensome in the long term.

To sum up, Google's competitiveness lies in its value proposition, revenue streams, and cost structures. Changes such as shifting its algorithm from keyword frequency based to reference based and including tools such as Google Analytics and geographic targeting systems make Google's services valuable. Improvements on Overture's model such as weighing bids and listing advertisements with their relevance taken into consideration improve the CTRs of advertisements and thus maximize Google's revenues. Reductions on cost such as developing their own server architecture support Google to scale sustainably as a business, eventually becoming the leader in the industry.
