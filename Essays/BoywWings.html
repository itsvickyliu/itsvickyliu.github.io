The Boy with Wings (Magical Realism)

Oct 2020

“Katie, come here. Meet your new classmate. This is Mark,” Mrs. Johnson called me. The boy standing next to her looked intimidated, bewildered, and shy, with his mouth struggling to form a smile and sweat dripping down his face. “I'm Katie.” I replied reluctantly. Who would like to be friends with an uncool kid? Mrs. Johnson frowned at me and said, “Mark, go sit at the desk next to Katie's.” That boy nodded and trudged towards me, avoiding curious glares from other students by staring at his shoes. What a terrible start for my first day of fifth grade! Unlike all my friends who would chat with each other when the teacher wasn't paying attention, that boy was unusually quiet. He had never talked to me or spoken in class. “You can't speak? Hello? Hello?” The boy looked startled, yet he still didn't say anything. Realizing that the boy couldn't talk made my entire fifth grade enjoyable. I began to treat him like a doll, taking away his sandwiches, hiding his textbook in restrooms, and breaking his pencils and erasers. Despite my tricks, he remained intimidated, bewildered, and shy like always. Until one day, I painted his dark blue pencil case with white paint. As soon as he saw the white paint dripping onto the pencil case, his lips started to tremble. An enormous pair of black feather wings grew behind his back within seconds. As he began to violently flap his wings, the whole classroom started to shake. I cried, “Stop Mike. Michael? Oh, Mark! You are destroying the building! Help!”
